The Art of Possible with Dynamic Quantum Networking: From Scalable Quantum Security to Quantum Data Centre Interconnect

November 10, 2023 | 2:00 p.m.
 

Special Collaborative Presentation: This week's seminar will be sponsored in-person on NC State's Centennial Campus and also hosted virtually by the IBM Quantum Innovation Center at NC State as part of the Triangle Quantum Computing Seminar Series.

The Duke Quantum Center, the IBM Quantum Innovation Center at NC State, and the UNC Kenan-Flagler's Rethinc. LABS are pleased to present the next Triangle Quantum Computing Seminar for Fall 2023 at 2 p.m., November 10, 2023. NC State welcomes Professor Reza Nejabati, head of the High-Performance Network group and head of Networks and Electronics Department in the School of Engineering at the University of Bristol, UK, to give a talk on "The Art of Possible with Dynamic Quantum Networking: From scalable quantum security to quantum data centre Interconnect."

Abstract: In this talk, Professor Reza Nejabati will delve into the fascinating realm of dynamic quantum networking. From scalable quantum security within existing telecom infrastructure to the seamless interconnection of quantum computers in a quantum data centre, he will address the challenges and provide practical solutions from both the physical layer and the control and management perspective.

Reza Nejabati

The Art of Possible with Dynamic Quantum Networking: From scalable quantum security to quantum data centre Interconnect

Professor Reza Nejabati is a Chair Professor, head of the High-Performance Network group and head of Networks and Electronics Department in the School of Engineering at the University of Bristol, UK. He is an experimental scientist, and his research vision is to develop a fully autonomous Internet infrastructure supporting communication and processing of quantum and classical information. He is leading large experimental research group focusing on quantum networking. He is also leading operation of large national quantum testbeds and experimental networks in the UK. He has a track record of pioneering research in quantum and classical network technologies. He has a successful track record in commercializing his research and utilising venture capital funding for spinning out his research. He co-founded Zeetta Networks to commercialise his research in Autonomous Optical Network.
