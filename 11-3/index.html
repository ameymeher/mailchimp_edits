Mutual Information Maximizing Quantum Generative Adversarial Network and Its Applications in Finance

November 3, 2023 | 2:00 p.m.

UNC Rethinc.Lab Hosting Seminar
 

The Duke Quantum Center, the IBM Quantum Innovation Center at NC State, and the UNC Kenan-Flagler's Rethinc. Labs are pleased to present the next Triangle Quantum Computing Seminar for Fall Semester 2023 at 2 p.m. November 3, 2023. The IBM Quantum Innovation Center at NC State welcomes Mingyu Lee, Myeongjin Shin and Harrison Junseo Lee of the UNC Kenan-Flagler's Rethinc. Labs, to give a talk on "Mutual Information Maximizing Quantum Generative Adversarial Network and Its Applications in Finance."

Abstract: We introduce a novel approach called InfoQGAN, which incorporates the Mutual Information Neural Estimator (MINE) into the framework of quantum generative adversarial networks to mitigate the mode collapse problem. Furthermore, we explain how this approach can be put into practice in a financial context, specifically dealing with the issue of generating portfolio return distributions through dynamic asset allocation. This demonstrates the potential real-world usefulness of InfoQGAN. For further details, please refer to https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.01363.
